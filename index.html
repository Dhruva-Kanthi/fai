About Us

The Finance and Investments (FAI) Club looks to empower students with the knowledge and skills to navigate the corporate world. FAI has evolved into a thriving 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to fostering financial literacy and education among students worldwide.

Available Opportunities
For You

At FAl, resources are continuously built up to meet members' desires. Our guest lectures continue each month, sharing some industry insights and allowing a special attending member Q&A. This year, chapters will share improved curriculum, featuring additional topics and interactive sessions. In addition, FAl is introducing exciting new competitions, namely virtual business challenges, entrepreneurship pitches, in addition to the annual stock market game. The introduction of FAl's Mentor Pool will allow students to connect with seasoned business professionals, turning to them for specific 1-to-1 guidance and advice. Lastly, the Research Pipeline program will provide a one of a kind opportunity for upperclassmen to collaborate with finance/economics professors during hands-on research projects.

FAI EVENTS

Stock Market Game

At FAl, we take pride in our Stock Market Game competition, an exciting and educational experience designed to immerse members of the Finance and Investments Club in the world of trading and investment. This interactive simulation allows participants to manage a virtual portfolio, make real-time decisions, and experience the thrills and challenges of the stock market.

FAI PHILANTHROPY

Around the World

As part of our philanthropic efforts, we have embarked on an ambitious project to build a library in a rural village in India. This initiative aims to provide access to educational resources and foster a culture of learning and literacy among the local community. With the collective efforts of our dedicated members and generous contributions from supporters, we have made significant progress toward this goal. Fundraising events, charity drives, and community outreach programs have helped us secure a substantial portion of the necessary funds.
